BY WALYCE ALMEIDA

Behind the Scenes with Yvonne Morales at the Channel Islands National Park

Operating a visitor-friendly, clean, and safe national park is a complicated task that requires many different specialists with a broad range of training and experience.

One of those specialists who often works behind the scenes is Yvonne Morales at the Channel Islands National Park in Southern California. She is a contract specialist and she helps hire the contractors who perform vital services and she also helps purchase the equipment and supplies the park needs every day.

“You name it, I bought it,” said Morales, a 30-year-old, Mexican-American stationed just a boat ride away in Ventura, California.

Park visitors might not often see her as they enjoy the building and sights of the Channel Islands, but they always see the results of her hard work.

For example, as the park's contract specialist, she recently contracted a company to renovate and build exhibits on an old Adobe ranch on Santa Cruz Island, one of the five islands.

"It's been the vision of the park to build a facility out there, a visitor center so that when visitors get off the shore of the island, they have a place to learn the history of the island," Morales said. "To me doing that job out there is fulfilling because I know it's going to impact visitors and employees that get to go out there and enjoy and experience that visitor center."

She enjoys seeing projects through to completion because it gives her a sense of fulfillment.

"To know that you had a small part in making that dream come alive for people who had envisioned that, that's just a feeling you never forget," Morales said.

Morales began working ten years ago at Channel Islands as an administrative assistant in the natural resources management department after studying business at a local college.

"I thought, 'Ok, this is something different. I've never worked for a park,'" she recalled. "But I wanted to get some experience."

What she found at the park was an embracing group of people and a worthwhile cause. She described her fellow employees as not only warm, but committed to the park and the visitors.

"We're a very diverse park in that we have different people from all walks of life," Morales said, adding that she keeps in touch with co-workers from different departments in the park that she described as more of a community than a mere workplace.

"There's so many opportunities in the park," Morales said. "I mean we have jobs from boat captains to scuba divers, to biologists, administrative positions, and things like that. So, it's a really cool place to work."

She said she enjoys the diversity of not only her co-workers and the visitors, but the resident wildlife as well.

“We preserve that diversity,” she said with a touch of excitement and obvious commitment.
